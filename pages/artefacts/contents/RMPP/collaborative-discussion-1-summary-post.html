Collaborative Discussion 1 - Summary Post


The Dark UX patterns are the design that takes advantage of users by providing misleading instructions deliberately (acm.org, 2018). Some websites play this trick to let users purchase additional products or services without intention (Nerdwriter1 2018).

In my first post (Yin Ping L. 2022.), I investigated the case study about the Dark UX patterns and its impacts and the way of mitigating the issue on computing ethics. The case study describes a scenario about the developer changing the design of an e-Commerce website, and it turns out their clients increased the revenue due to providing ambiguous instructions (ACM 2018.). I also listed out the differences between ACM Code of Ethics (acm.org, 2021) and BCS Code of conduct (bcs.org, 2021) while analyzing this case study. Both of them are providing similar and comprehensive guidelines for computing professionals to build up a high standard of ethics in the IT industry. In the final section, I provided recommendations for dealing with this situation, such as reporting to the superiors and speaking out loud in the society.

Shota Kameyama (2022) pointed out an interesting view that the change request can be considered as illegal and breaching the law of the UKâ€™s Fraud Act 2006 (Fraud Act 2006, 2007). In this case study, the clients are actually gaining profits due to user error. Therefore, it can be considered as fraud by providing misleading incorrect information in order to increase revenue.

To sum up, the ACM Code of Ethics and BCS Code of Conduct are guidelines for computing professionals, but they do not have any effects in legislation. Therefore, they cannot avoid developers to implement the Dark UX patterns on the website.
