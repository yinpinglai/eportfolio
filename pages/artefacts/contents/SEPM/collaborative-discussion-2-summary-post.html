Collaborative Discussion 2 - Summary Post


User experience is an abstract topic on finding the way to establish an environment that fulfill user’s needs and feels through the interaction between them (Nick B., 2020). The CUE model categorized those components into three categories: instrumental qualities, non-instrumental qualities, and emotion reactions. They are both affecting each other. So, users can be changed their minds during different stages (Minge and Thüring, 2018).

While revisited my initial post and watched peers’ posts, we agreed that the CUE model wasn’t a complete picture for measuring the user experience for all users. We suggested that the author should add the persona into the criteria (Kei Yiu Yvone Chan, 2022) because different age, gender, and race people might have different results while using the good-looking product (Nate G., 2022).

To sum up, the user experience is an abstract topic and hard to be measured. As there are too many factors to affect the user experience, such as priori experience on similar products/services, peer reviews, etc. Consequently, the results can be vary among different group of people.
